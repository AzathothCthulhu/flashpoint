X. THE WAR ROOM
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

X.1  Design Rationale
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every real major incident has a war room. It is the physical or virtual space
where senior leaders converge to share situational awareness, surface conflicts
between workstreams, escalate decisions that exceed any single role's authority,
and maintain a unified command picture across an otherwise fragmented response.

Without a war room, incident response degenerates into silos. The CISO optimises
for forensic completeness while Legal is strategically delaying notification to
protect privilege, and Comms is making public statements that contradict both.
Each team is operating rationally within their domain; collectively, the
organisation is in chaos.

The war room in FLASHPOINT is designed to simulate this exact tension. It is not
simply a dashboard — it is a shared situational awareness layer that makes
cross-role conflict visible, names the decisions that require multi-role
alignment, and tracks whether the team is converging or fragmenting.

The war room is the game's primary vehicle for teaching that a data breach is
not a technical problem, a legal problem, a communications problem, or a
financial problem. It is all of those simultaneously, and the role of senior
leadership is to hold the whole picture at once.


X.2  Mechanical Role in the Game
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The war room operates as a shared overlay accessible to all players. It surfaces
information that no single role can see in their own domain view.

Individual role views enforce information asymmetry: each player sees their own
domain pools in full and other roles' status only as aggregate health signals
(strong / mixed / poor). This models the reality that a CFO does not have
moment-to-moment visibility into the CISO's forensic team morale, and the CISO
does not know the precise legal exposure calculation Legal is running.

The war room breaks this asymmetry intentionally and deliberately. When a player
opens the war room, they see:

  SITUATION STRIP
  Seven headline numbers drawn from shared resource pools: Regulatory Exposure,
  Intelligence Quality, Public Trust, Reputation, Liquidity Pressure, Board
  Confidence, and Crisis Containment. These are the metrics every role is
  simultaneously affecting through their daily actions. Seeing them together,
  for the first time, often produces the game's most significant learning
  moments — a CISO who has been investing heavily in forensics discovers that
  Reputation has been collapsing because Comms had no budget to invest in the
  media workstream.

  ROLE PRESENCE BOARD
  Every role's current status: ready / acting, AP spent vs budget, health
  signal, and whether they have unread cross-role messages. This surfaces
  coordination failures — three roles marked ready but one is still deliberating
  creates visible pressure, as does a role with 0 AP spent (a signal that either
  they have not engaged with the day's choices, or they are strategically
  hoarding points).

  ESCALATION QUEUE
  Ten conditional escalation rules evaluate against current game state every
  time the war room is opened. When triggered, they appear as prioritised items
  requiring CEO arbitration. Escalations represent decisions that cross role
  boundaries — a forensics spend that has created an unplanned liquidity event
  (CISO → CFO), an NDB notification delay that has breached the ethical tension
  threshold (Legal → CEO), a board briefing window approaching with no
  coordinated position (CEO → All), a public statement that contradicts the
  internal technical timeline (Comms → CISO). These are not failure states;
  they are the natural consequence of independent domain decisions intersecting.
  The game rewards teams that catch and resolve escalations early.

  CROSS-ROLE ACTIVITY FEED
  A time-stamped log of all domain actions taken across all roles. Individual
  players can only see their own action log; the war room aggregates these into
  a single feed, enabling the CEO or any facilitator to reconstruct what each
  role actually did on a given day.

  WORKSTREAM PROGRESS
  The five workstreams (forensics, regulatory, media, financial, stakeholder)
  aggregate investment from all roles. A workstream only reaches its threshold
  payoffs if multiple roles coordinate investment — no single role has enough
  AP to fund a workstream to 100% alone. The war room is the only place all
  five workstream bars appear together, making resource allocation tradeoffs
  visible as a collective decision rather than a series of independent ones.

  GATE-IN-FLIGHT PANEL
  When a decision gate is active, the war room shows each role's stated
  position. This enables the CEO to identify contested decisions before they
  are committed and to call for explicit alignment rather than discovering a
  split outcome after the fact.


X.3  The War Room and Learning Outcomes
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The war room is designed to support three core learning outcomes that cannot be
achieved through individual role play alone:

  1. SYSTEMS THINKING — CONSEQUENCES CROSS BOUNDARIES
     Players who see only their role view experience consequences as mysterious.
     Why did Legal Exposure jump when I didn't make a Legal decision? The war
     room reveals that a Comms public statement (G7: Option C) triggered a
     cascade into Regulatory Exposure. Seeing the shared pool state as a
     collective responsibility — not a background threat — is the shift the
     war room is designed to produce.

  2. COORDINATION UNDER PRESSURE IS NOT AUTOMATIC
     Even when everyone is acting competently within their domain, the team can
     fragment. The escalation queue makes this failure mode legible. Teams that
     debrief with the war room visible understand why the outcome diverged from
     their intent — not because anyone made a bad decision, but because the
     decisions were never reconciled into a unified response.

  3. THE CEO ROLE IS STRUCTURAL, NOT SYMBOLIC
     In most tabletop exercises, the CEO is a title without mechanical weight.
     In FLASHPOINT, the CEO is the only role with authority to override a day
     advance when roles are split, to resolve escalated cross-role conflicts,
     and to absorb Reputation pool impacts on behalf of the organisation. The
     war room is where the CEO's structural role becomes operationally visible:
     it is not enough to set strategy; the CEO must actively monitor the shared
     picture and intervene before cascade thresholds trigger.


X.4  War Room Design Constraints
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The war room must be kept out of the individual role view by design. If players
can always see cross-role information, the information asymmetry mechanic
collapses and the simulation loses its primary tension. The war room is accessed
explicitly — in the prototype, via a toolbar button; in the facilitated mockup,
via a view toggle — and this friction is intentional.

Escalation rules are evaluated dynamically against current game state, not
pre-scripted. This means the escalation queue reflects the actual decisions the
team has made, not a predetermined narrative. A team that resolves G2 as Option
A (immediate NDB notification) will never see the ESC-02 escalation (NDB clock
held / ethical tension accumulating). The escalation queue is a mirror, not a
script.

The activity feed is append-only and never edits or deletes entries. This models
the incident log reality: actions taken are on record, regardless of subsequent
decisions. This is particularly important for the post-exercise debrief, where
the facilitator can reconstruct the full decision sequence from the war room feed
rather than relying on player recollection.


X.5  Escalation Rules Reference
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The following ten escalation rules are implemented in the current prototype.
Each rule has an ID, urgency level, originating role, destination role, and a
conditional trigger evaluated against current pool state, decision log, and day.

  ESC-01  SPEND      HIGH    CISO → CFO
  Forensics spend unreconciled with CFO
  Trigger: G3 committed as A or B AND G8 not yet decided.
  Rationale: External forensics engagement has created an unplanned liquidity
  event that requires CFO visibility before the next day advance.

  ESC-02  CONFLICT   HIGH    Legal → CEO
  NDB clock held — ethical tension accumulating
  Trigger: G2 committed as B (hold notification) AND Day ≥ 5 AND
  LEGAL.ETHICAL_TENSION > 45.
  Rationale: Sustained notification delay has pushed Legal into territory where
  the strategic decision conflicts with ethical obligations. CEO arbitration
  required.

  ESC-03  INTEL      MEDIUM  CISO → Legal
  Forensics evidence not yet shared with Legal
  Trigger: G3 committed AND G5 not yet decided.
  Rationale: Legal cannot assess privilege scope without visibility into
  forensic evidence chain. Cross-role INTEL_SHARE pending.

  ESC-04  COMMS      HIGH    Comms → CEO
  Public statement window — no coordinated position
  Trigger: Day ≥ 7 AND G7 not yet decided AND PUBLIC_TRUST < 35.
  Rationale: Media pressure has reached a threshold requiring a public
  statement. No cross-role position has been established. Reputational
  cascade risk.

  ESC-05  BOARD      HIGH    CEO → All
  Board briefing window — preparation incomplete
  Trigger: G6 not yet decided AND BOARD_CONFIDENCE < 40.
  Rationale: Board confidence has degraded to a level that will trigger
  governance intervention if not addressed before end of Act 1.

  ESC-06  SPEND      MEDIUM  CFO → CEO
  Liquidity threshold approaching — budget reconciliation needed
  Trigger: CFO.BUDGET_INTEGRITY < 40 AND G8 not yet decided.
  Rationale: Budget integrity below threshold indicates unreconciled
  commitments. CEO sign-off required before further spend authorisation.

  ESC-07  CONFLICT   MEDIUM  Comms → Legal
  Media narrative conflicts with legal hold position
  Trigger: G7 committed AND G2 committed as B.
  Rationale: Public communications have been made that are potentially
  inconsistent with the decision to delay NDB notification. Privilege
  exposure risk.

  ESC-08  INTEL      LOW     CISO → Comms
  Technical timeline not shared with Comms
  Trigger: G3 committed AND G7 not yet decided.
  Rationale: Comms cannot make accurate public statements about the incident
  scope without access to the technical forensic timeline.

  ESC-09  SPEND      HIGH    Legal → CFO
  External counsel spend — board approval threshold approaching
  Trigger: LEGAL.LEGAL_EXPOSURE > 60 AND G8 not yet decided.
  Rationale: Legal exposure has reached a level where external counsel spend
  will approach the board approval threshold. Finance must be informed.

  ESC-10  CONFLICT   HIGH    All → CEO
  Multi-role conflict — containment strategy split
  Trigger: Day ≥ 10 AND CRISIS_CONTAINMENT < 30 AND
  REGULATORY_EXPOSURE > 55.
  Rationale: Crisis containment has failed to the point where regulatory
  exposure is critical. The team's containment strategy is no longer aligned.
  CEO must establish unified command position.
