The Scenario

Third Party — Meridian Health

Day 0, 06:22. CISO monitoring logs flag an unusual 4.3GB export from the NovaCare integration — a third-party healthcare data platform Meridian uses to manage patient appointment records. Within hours, security researcher Claire Ashworth emails the CISO with a 40-record sample of what appears to be authentic Meridian patient data, now circulating on a dark web forum. She is publishing in 48 hours.

You will run Meridian Health's executive response across 21 days and three Acts — making decisions as CISO, CFO, General Counsel, Communications Lead, and CEO. Each decision changes resource pools, triggers cross-role consequences, and opens or forecloses narrative branches. There is no correct answer. There are three possible endings. Which one you reach depends entirely on the decisions you commit.

Senior physician Dr. Sarah Obi is among those whose records may be in the exposed sample. How you treat her — and when — is one of the most consequential threads in the scenario.

The Three Acts
ACT 1 — EXPOSURE · Days 1–7
The incident is discovered and assessed. Initial response posture is set. Forensics are engaged or not. The board is briefed at some level. The NDB clock question is live. Staff are notified or not. The world does not yet know.
ACT 2 — ESCALATION · Days 8–15
Ashworth publishes. Media picks it up. Attribution must be decided. The regulator makes contact. The remediation budget is authorised. Strategic posture is set. Cross-role consequences compound and become visible.
ACT 3 — RESOLUTION · Days 16–21
The shape of the ending is now determined by prior play. Dr. Obi's trajectory resolves. CEO accountability is decided. The final public narrative closes the scenario. The debrief surfaces what the run produced.
The Roles
CISO
Incident lead. Owns Security Posture, Control Framework, Audit Readiness, Team Morale, and Executive Trust. Decides response posture, forensics, and technical attribution.
CFO
Financial lead. Owns Liquidity, Budget Integrity, Financial Reporting Risk. Authorises or declines emergency spend — the single most visible cross-role action in the game.
General Counsel
Legal and regulatory lead. Owns Legal Exposure, Privilege Integrity, Regulatory Relationships, Ethical Tension. Decides the NDB clock, disclosure scope, and regulator posture.
Comms / PR Lead
Narrative lead. Owns Message Control, Narrative Coherence, Stakeholder Confidence, Media Relationships. Decides every external statement. Every statement creates a legal surface.
CEO + HR
Executive and people lead. Decides board briefing scope, internal notification, strategic posture, CEO accountability, and the final public narrative.
Reading the Interface
◧
Left Panel — Resource Pools
All 27 resource pools, grouped by owner: Shared (organisation-wide), CISO, CFO, Legal, Comms, and Scenario-specific. Each pool shows its current value as a number and a colour-coded bar. Values update instantly when you commit a decision. Watch this panel to see what your decisions are actually doing to the organisation.
⬤
Centre Panel — Decision Gates
Decision gates appear here on their scheduled day. Each gate shows the scenario context, the deciding role(s), and two or three choices. Hover a choice to preview its pool effects before committing. Click Commit to lock in the decision — it cannot be undone. Gates with a green ✓ are completed. Gates with a filled dot are active today.
≡
Right Panel — Log & Branches
Two tabs. Decision Log records every pool change — gate, delta, pool name, visibility tag, and the note explaining what caused it. Read this to trace cause and effect. Branches shows all 40 narrative branches: ✓ open (your decision matched), ✗ foreclosed (a different choice was made), ○ not yet decided.
▤
Bottom Centre — Timeline
The scenario event timeline shows scheduled injects and state-triggered events near the current day. 🌙 overnight events are morning-anxiety events that fire during low-activity periods. Warning events are state-conditioned — they only fire if specific pool thresholds are crossed. Fired events are highlighted in blue.
▊
Bottom Right — Ending Probability
A live estimate of which ending you are tracking toward based on your committed decisions and current pool values. Ending A (green) is the best-case resolution, reached by roughly 10–15% of runs. B (yellow) is the most common. C (red) is the worst-case, reached by 25–40% of runs. Updated after every committed decision.
⬡
Debrief Button
Opens the narrative debrief at any point in the run — not just at Day 21. The debrief generates a role-by-role analysis of what went right and wrong, identifies the key turning points in the run, surfaces cross-role consequences that would have been hidden from individual players, and delivers a final verdict on the scenario outcome.

Making Decisions
1
Advance the day using the Advance Day ▶ button in the header. Each click applies daily decay to TIME and Intelligence Quality, fires any scheduled events for that day, and reveals active decision gates.
2
Read the gate context. Each decision gate shows which role is deciding, the scenario situation, and two or three choices. The context is drawn directly from the game design document — it reflects the real information the role would have.
3
Hover a choice to preview its pool effects before committing. Effects show the delta value, the pool name, the visibility tag (when it would appear to players), and whether the cause would be hidden from the affected role.
4
Click Commit to lock in the decision. Consequences apply immediately to the pool state. The decision is logged, branches are updated, and the ending probability recalculates. Committed decisions cannot be undone — by design.
5
Check the Decision Log after committing to see every pool delta produced — including hidden cross-role effects that individual players would not normally see. Toggle Show hidden effects in the header to control what the preview shows.
6
Open the Debrief at any time — mid-run or at Day 21 — for the full narrative analysis. Run the scenario multiple times with different choices to map the consequence space.
Effect Visibility & Attribution

Every pool effect carries two tags that describe what players in a real multiplayer run would — and would not — see.

IMMEDIATEEffect applies the moment the decision is committed — visible in the same session.
NEXT_LOGINEffect is queued — the affected role sees it when they next log in. Simulates overnight consequences.
ACT_BOUNDARYEffect materialises at the next live checkpoint session — end of Days 7, 15, or 19.
ENDEffect only becomes visible at scenario close — long-tail consequences of decisions made weeks earlier.
FULL_DISCLOSUREAll roles see cause and effect — this is a visible, shared consequence.
CAUSE_VISIBLEThe affected role sees the effect and knows what caused it.
CAUSE_HIDDEN ●The affected role sees their pool move but cannot see what caused it. This is the engine's information asymmetry in action — the drama of not knowing why your domain is under pressure.
Cascade Rules

Four threshold conditions trigger automatic cascade effects — they fire silently when pools cross their limits.

TIME < 25 — Accelerated drain on Capacity (−5) and Intelligence Quality (−3). Time pressure degrades organisational function.
REPUTATION ≤ −20 — Regulatory Standing takes an additional −5 hit. A damaged public reputation makes the regulator less forgiving.
REGULATORY_STANDING ≤ −20 — Capacity takes a −8 hit. Legal capacity is consumed by formal proceedings.
INTELLIGENCE_QUALITY < 30 — Decision outcomes enter a ±20% noise band. Decisions feel "unlucky" — correct choices produce weaker results, poor choices produce worse ones.
Solo testing mode. This prototype is designed for a single tester running all roles. A few things to know: (1) In full multiplayer play, each role sees only their own domain pools and shared pools — here you see everything. Use the Show hidden effects toggle to simulate what individual roles would experience. (2) Committed decisions are final — there is no undo. Use ↺ Reset to restart cleanly and explore a different decision path. (3) The debrief works at any point in the run, not just at the end — use it mid-scenario to understand the consequences of decisions made so far. (4) Delta values are calibrated so no single decision depletes any pool below 50% of its range in isolation — catastrophic states require compound poor decisions across multiple gates.
